# Skill: trip

Allowed kinds: `arrange`, `prep`, `pack`, `checkin`.
- **arrange** — earliest logistics: pet/house sitter, rides, parking, PTO,
  lodging, bookings, passports, childcare, or house prep.
- **prep** — a few days out: itinerary, documents, tickets, chargers, medication,
  weather, reservations, or destination essentials.
- **pack** — packing clothes/toiletries/gear before leaving.
- **checkin** — flight check-in, hotel check-in, rental car confirmation, or
  day-before travel confirmations.

Guidance:
- Multi-day does not automatically mean trip. Use trip only with travel/away
  cues: destination, lodging, flights, vacation, road trip, camping, airport,
  conference travel, or "in <city/place>".
- Flights: prefer `checkin` + `pack`.
- Longer trips/vacations: prefer `arrange` + `prep`.
- Hotel/Airbnb-only events: usually `checkin` + `prep`.
- Conferences away from home: `arrange` + `prep`; local conferences may be
  `generic` or ignored unless prep is obvious.
- If the event is a holiday/wedding/anniversary with travel, the router may use
  that category instead; do not force trip just because travel is involved.

Titles: "Pack for Denver", "Check in for the flight", "Arrange a sitter for the trip", "Prep the travel itinerary".
